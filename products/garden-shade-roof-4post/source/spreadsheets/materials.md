# Materials List — DIY Garden Shade Roof (Four-Post Sloped)

> Quantities are estimates for a ~8'x10' footprint. Adjust to your bed
> dimensions. Use **pressure-treated lumber for any in-ground or
> ground-contact members**.

## Posts & Footings
- (4) 4x4 PT — 10 ft (cut to ~7 ft front / ~6 ft back after setting)
- (4) bags fast-setting concrete (or your local equivalent), or
- (4) above-ground post anchors with appropriate hardware
- Gravel for drainage at footing base

## Beams & Rafters
- (2) 2x6 SPF — 12 ft (long front and back beams)
- (5) 2x4 SPF — 10 ft (rafters spanning beams)
- (4) 2x4 SPF — 4 ft (diagonal knee braces)

## Roof Cover (choose one)
**Shade-cloth version:**
- (1) shade cloth panel sized 9' x 11' (40–60% shade rating typical)
- (1) bag stainless or galvanized cable ties / grommet hardware

**Corrugated panel version:**
- (4) corrugated polycarbonate or metal panels — ~26" x 96"
- (1) box panel screws with rubber washers
- (1) sealing strip kit

## Hardware
- (8) 3/8" lag bolts — 4" (beam-to-post)
- (1) box 3" exterior screws (rafters, braces)
- (1) tube exterior caulk

## Optional
- Removable side curtains (extra shade cloth + carabiners).
- Drip-line gutter to redirect runoff.

## Notes
- Sloped roofs catch wind. Anchor posts properly; consider buried-and-concreted
  footings if your area sees gusts > 40 mph.
- Drain runoff away from raised beds and foundations.
