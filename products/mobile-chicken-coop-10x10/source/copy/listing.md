- Ground footprint: 10' x 10' outside frame.
- Lower run: 10' x 10' footprint.
- Lower run height: 48" (mobile).
- Upper coop house: 4' x 10' outside footprint.
- Upper coop interior area: about 40 sq ft before framing loss.
- Upper coop wall heights: 48" high side / 40" low side.
- Roof: lightweight sloped roof with ~8" fall across 48" depth.
- Posts: nine 4x4 PT posts (3 front / 3 coop-front / 3 rear).
- Wire: 1/2" 19-ga galvanized hardware cloth.
- Mobility: two 4x6 PT skids + axle-mounted pneumatic wheels + tow tongue.

## Capacity Guidance
- **Layers:** 10–12 hens (recommended).

**Q: Can I use this for broilers instead of layers?**
A: Yes. Broiler mode uses the lower run as the primary space and removes nest boxes and roosts. The PDF includes a full 8-week grow-out schedule.

**Q: Do I need a permit?**
A: Permitting depends on your jurisdiction. Check with your local building department before building.

**Q: What predators does it resist?**
A: Plans use 1/2" 19-ga galvanized hardware cloth, lath screws + fender washers (or batten strips), two-step latch guidance, and an optional fold-out apron. No coop is fully predator-proof; verify your local pressure (raccoons, foxes, dogs, hawks, snakes, rats, etc.) and adjust. Do not use chicken wire for predator protection.

**Q: Will this withstand my snow load / wind load?**

A: The mobile version uses a 48" lower run height to keep the center of gravity lower for pasture movement. A heavier walk-in variant should be treated as a separate ATV/UTV/tractor-towed redesign for flatter ground.
